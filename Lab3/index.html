Student Clock First Brainstorm

Product scope, dashboard rules, and MVP architecture

Date: April 14, 2026

Attendance

  • Rushil Gupta (Team 16)
  • Shruti Senthilram
  • Sanjana Kalarickal
  • Benjamin Resnick
  • Derek Wadsworth
  • Neal Jian

Agenda

  1. Confirm the problem space: coursework organization, deadlines, and study-hour realism
  2. Brainstorm core entities: terms, courses, series, and assignments
  3. Draft dashboard rules for “urgent” work (due soon, behind schedule, effective due dates)
  4. Sketch MVP direction: JavaFX UI, SQLite persistence, MVP layering (Model–View–Presenter)

Unfinished business (from last meeting)

  • Whether “Series” should own late policies and ordering for related assignments — agreed: yes, series groups share policies and can enforce sequential expectations.
  • How to define “behind schedule” for progress indicators — carry over: tie to estimated time, logged work, and time until due; Rushil to propose a first formula draft.

New business

  • Prototype weekly study-hour configuration and “remaining hours this week” behavior in the model.
  • Decide minimum fields for assignments (estimated time, start date, due date) and validation rules.
  • Outline the Big Picture visualization: what axes, what density is readable, and how it relates to the term overview.

Comments, questions, and concerns

  • Shruti asked how manual time entry and work logging stay consistent with dashboard urgency if users forget to log sessions.
  • Benjamin raised TestFX and UI stability: we should prefer stable node IDs over brittle selectors early.
  • Sanjana suggested documenting repository boundaries so SQLite details do not leak into presenters.
